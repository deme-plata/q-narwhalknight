### 5. Polish Technical Dialogue in Chapter 3
**Current Issue**: Some exposition feels slightly info-dump-y

**Scene 2 Revisions** (Phoenix's briefing):

**Before**:
```markdown
"The community reviewed what they were allowed to see," Yuki pulled up a dense mathematical proof. "The backdoors are in the parameter generation—specific choices of polynomials and reduction bases that look random but aren't. I was coerced into designing them by a consortium that wanted insurance against post-quantum security."
```

**After**:
```markdown
"The community reviewed what they were shown," Yuki said, pulling up a dense mathematical proof. "See this?" She pointed to a section of lattice reduction parameters. "This polynomial basis looks random. Every reviewer thought so. But it's not."

Marcus leaned closer. "How can you tell?"

"Because I designed it to look random." Yuki's voice was bitter. "Kronos gave me a choice in 2021: embed their trapdoor, or watch my research funding disappear and my security clearance get revoked. I thought I could expose it later, find a way to warn people. But they were always three steps ahead."

Elena felt sick. "So every Crystals-Kyber implementation since 2024..."

"Has a backdoor that only works if you know the secret reduction algorithm. Which Kronos does. Because I gave it to them."
```

**Impact**:
- Breaks up exposition with dialogue
- Shows character emotion alongside technical detail
- Makes backstory feel more personal
- Maintains technical accuracy while improving flow
