## The Journey

**Programming**: From web applications to distributed systems, I learned that code is truth. No PowerPoint presentations, no corporate buzzwords—just systems that either work or don't.

**Quantum Physics**: While most developers ignored quantum mechanics as "theoretical," I studied Shor's algorithm, quantum key distribution, and post-quantum cryptography. The quantum threat wasn't a distant possibility—it was inevitable.

**Key Insight**: The blockchain revolution would eventually collide with the quantum revolution. Someone needed to prepare.

### Advanced Systems (2015-2022): Deep Dive

**Distributed Consensus**: Byzantine fault tolerance, DAG structures, gossip protocols. Built production systems handling millions of users before "blockchain" was mainstream.

**Quantum Research**: Studied lattice-based cryptography, post-quantum signature schemes, and quantum threat models. Read every paper on Dilithium, Kyber, and SPHINCS+.

**Key Insight**: Post-quantum migration can't be an afterthought. It must be architectural from day one.

### Business Education + Entrepreneurial Pivot (2022-2025)

**Copenhagen Business School**: Three years studying Information Technology and Business Economics at CBS in Frederiksberg. Learned SWOT analysis, business models, organizational theory.

**The Realization**: Business schools teach case studies of companies that succeeded. But you don't learn to build by studying those who built—you learn by building.

**The Decision**: After three years, I chose startup execution over academic theory. Not because I couldn't finish, but because the classroom couldn't keep up with what I needed to build.

**Key Insight**: The best MBA is building a company. The best teacher is failure. The best case study is your own.

### Q-NarwhalKnight (2024-2025): Theory Meets Reality

- Do you have runway? (Savings, side income, or revenue)
